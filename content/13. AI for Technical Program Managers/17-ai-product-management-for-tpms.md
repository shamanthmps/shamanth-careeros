# AI Product Management for TPMs

## Executive Summary
AI product management for TPMs focuses on problem-first execution, measurable value, and controlled rollout. Interviewers expect you to avoid "AI feature theater" and articulate clear user outcomes.

Strong candidates tie model quality metrics to product KPIs and adoption behavior.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Bridges product intent and technical feasibility.
- Architecture relevance: Product requirements shape evaluation and guardrails.
- Interview relevance: Common behavioral + design crossover topic.

## Core Concepts
### Product Foundations
- Problem statement and user pain.
- Success metrics (quality + business).
- UX for uncertainty (confidence, citations, fallback).
- Adoption and feedback loops.

### Internal Working
Discover -> prototype -> evaluate -> limited rollout -> iterate -> scale.

### ASCII Flow
User Need -> AI Feature Hypothesis -> Eval -> Launch Gate -> Iteration

### Trade-offs
- Broad feature scope vs focused high-value use case.
- Fast launch vs quality confidence.

## Real-world Example
Sales copilot:
- Initial broad assistant had weak usage.
- Refocused on proposal drafting and objection handling.
- Adoption improved due to narrower high-value scope.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What user task improves measurably?
- How will trust be built in UX?
- What confidence threshold drives human review?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I define a narrow high-value AI use case, set quality and business metrics together, design UX for uncertainty, and roll out in stages with user feedback loops."

## Common Mistakes
- Launching broad assistant with unclear value.
- No UX for uncertain output.
- Measuring engagement only, not task outcomes.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Start with concrete user pain.
- Define quality + business KPIs.
- Design trust-aware UX and phased rollout.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you identify the right first AI feature?
2. What product metrics matter for AI copilot success?
3. How do you design UX for uncertain outputs?
4. How do you run AI feature A/B experiments?
5. How do you sunset low-value AI features?

## Key Takeaways
- AI product success is outcome-driven, not novelty-driven.
- Trust and usability are core product requirements.
- TPMs should enforce metric discipline from MVP.
