# AI Program Management & Governance

## Executive Summary
AI program management coordinates model, data, product, security, legal, and operations tracks into one governed delivery program. Interviewers expect TPMs to show governance mechanisms, not just project plans.

Strong answers include decision forums, risk taxonomy, launch gates, and accountability.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: AI programs span many high-risk dependencies.
- Architecture relevance: Governance decisions affect architecture constraints.
- Interview relevance: Principal-level leadership signal.

## Core Concepts
### Governance Components
- Risk-tiering framework
- Model/prompt/version change control
- Evaluation and safety release gates
- Incident response and escalation paths
- Audit and compliance artifacts

### Internal Working
Roadmap planning -> governance checkpoints -> launch gate -> post-launch review loop.

### ASCII Loop
Plan -> Build -> Evaluate -> Approve -> Launch -> Audit -> Improve

### Trade-offs
- Strong governance: safer launches, slower cycle time.
- Loose governance: faster changes, higher operational risk.

## Real-world Example
Enterprise AI assistant rollout:
- Created AI review board with product, security, legal, and SRE.
- Introduced risk-tiered launch criteria.
- Reduced post-launch incidents and policy violations.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Which risk tier is this feature?
- What evidence is required for approval?
- Who owns each control and SLA?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I run AI governance as a risk-based operating model with clear release gates, ownership, and audit trails, balancing iteration speed with safety and compliance obligations."

## Common Mistakes
- Governance by ad-hoc meetings only.
- No ownership for controls.
- No post-launch audit loop.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Governance needs explicit risk tiers and gates.
- Define owners for every control.
- Include post-launch audits and incident learning.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. What should an AI launch gate include?
2. How do you set governance tiers by risk?
3. How do you prevent governance from becoming bottleneck?
4. What artifacts are needed for audit readiness?
5. How do you handle urgent model rollback decisions?

## Key Takeaways
- AI governance is an operating system, not a document.
- Risk-tiered controls enable both speed and safety.
- TPMs should institutionalize review cadence and accountability.
