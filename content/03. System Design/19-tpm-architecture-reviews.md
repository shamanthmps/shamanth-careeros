# TPM Architecture Reviews

## Executive Summary
TPM architecture reviews ensure technical decisions are aligned with business goals, delivery plans, and risk controls. In interviews, this topic tests your ability to lead architecture governance without becoming a bottleneck.

Strong candidates show how they drive quality through clear criteria, review cadence, and decision documentation.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Reviews prevent late-stage failures and rework.
- Architecture relevance: Improves consistency across teams and systems.
- System design relevance: Senior TPMs are expected to run effective architecture forums.

## Core Concepts
### Review Pillars
- Requirement clarity
- Scalability and reliability posture
- Security and compliance readiness
- Cost and operational sustainability
- Execution readiness and ownership

### Internal Working
Review flow:
1. Pre-read with architecture template.
2. Structured review with decision criteria.
3. Action items with owners and dates.
4. Follow-up checkpoints.

### ASCII Governance Loop
Proposal -> Review -> Decision -> Actions -> Re-review

### Trade-offs
- Strong governance vs review overhead
- Central review board vs team autonomy

## Real-world Example
Cross-team checkout redesign review:
- Identified missing rollback plan and unclear owner for data migration.
- Added readiness gate before launch.
- Avoided high-risk cutover.

## TPM Perspective
Questions to enforce:
- Are assumptions explicit and validated?
- What is the top failure mode and mitigation?
- Is ownership clear for runbooks and alerts?
- What is go/no-go checklist?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I run architecture reviews with standardized criteria, pre-reads, and explicit decision records. I focus on high-risk assumptions, ownership, and launch readiness gates to reduce execution surprises."

## Common Mistakes
- Design reviews with no decision criteria.
- No action tracking post-review.
- Architecture approval without rollback readiness.
- Security and cost ignored until late stages.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Use a consistent review rubric.
- Make decisions and assumptions explicit.
- Track actions and readiness gates.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you structure architecture reviews at scale?
2. What should be mandatory in pre-read docs?
3. How do you avoid review bottlenecks?
4. How do you handle disagreement in architecture forums?
5. What metrics show review effectiveness?

## Key Takeaways
- Architecture reviews are risk management mechanisms.
- Standardization improves quality and speed.
- Decision logs and action tracking are essential.
