# Go-to-Market Basics

## Executive Summary
Go-to-market (GTM) strategy defines how a product reaches its target customers. TPMs are responsible for the technical readiness of launches and increasingly for coordinating across product, marketing, sales, and support to deliver a coherent launch.

In interviews, GTM questions evaluate your cross-functional coordination and launch execution discipline.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Launch failures are rarely due to code quality — they are coordination failures.
- Architecture relevance: Launch-readiness includes scalability, monitoring, and rollback.
- Interview relevance: Common in execution and cross-functional leadership rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- GTM strategy: Plan for delivering value to target customers through the right channels.
- Launch tiers: Classification of launch size and cross-functional coordination required.
- Enablement: Equipping sales, support, and marketing with product knowledge.
- Rollback plan: Technical and communication plan for post-launch reversal.

### Internal Working
Launch tier defined -> workstreams identified -> owners assigned -> readiness checklist -> staged launch -> post-launch review.

### ASCII Diagram
Feature Ready -> Launch Tier -> Workstreams -> Readiness Gate -> Launch -> Post-Launch Review

### Trade-offs
- Full coordinated launch vs fast incremental ship.
- Broad market launch vs limited beta with feedback loop.

## Real-world Example
A platform team launched without coordinating support enablement. Support ticket volume spiked within 24 hours because agents lacked product knowledge. Post-launch review added support readiness as a mandatory launch gate.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What launch tier is this and what workstreams are required?
- Is support enabled and do they have self-service content?
- What is the go/no-go criteria and who has authority to block?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I define launch tier based on scope and risk, assign workstream owners across product, engineering, support, and marketing, enforce a readiness checklist with authority to delay, and run a post-launch review on every significant release."

## Common Mistakes
- Equating code deployment with product launch.
- No support or sales enablement.
- No post-launch review cadence.

## 5-Minute Revision
- GTM is cross-functional launch coordination.
- Launch tier determines coordination scope.
- Readiness gate + rollback plan are mandatory.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you define launch tiers?
2. What workstreams are required for a major product launch?
3. How do you manage launch delays?
4. What is in a rollback plan?
5. How do you run post-launch reviews effectively?

## Key Takeaways
- Product launches are programs, not deployments.
- Cross-functional coordination determines launch quality.
- TPMs own launch execution and readiness accountability.
