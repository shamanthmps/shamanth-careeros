# Roadmap Strategy

## Executive Summary
A roadmap is a strategic communication tool, not a Gantt chart. In TPM interviews, roadmap questions test your ability to sequence delivery under uncertainty while maintaining stakeholder confidence and team clarity.

Strong answers show you can balance commitments, discovery, and technical health investment across time horizons.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Roadmap quality determines stakeholder trust and team focus.
- Architecture relevance: Platform and infrastructure needs must be visible in roadmaps.
- Interview relevance: Common scenario and behavioral topic.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Roadmap: A time-oriented view of planned outcomes and investments.
- Outcome-based roadmap: Organized by goals, not features.
- Now/Next/Later: A lightweight three-horizon framework.
- Theme: A strategic cluster of related work.

### Internal Working
Strategic context -> themes -> outcomes -> sequenced delivery bets -> communication to stakeholders.

### ASCII Diagram
Now (committed) | Next (planned) | Later (directional)

### Trade-offs
- Detailed roadmaps feel trustworthy but become stale quickly.
- Outcome-based roadmaps age better but may feel vague to stakeholders.

## Real-world Example
A TPM switched from a feature-list roadmap to an outcome-based roadmap. Stakeholder alignment improved because teams could focus on "reduce onboarding drop-off by 30%" rather than debating individual features.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Does the roadmap communicate outcomes or just tasks?
- How much of the roadmap is committed vs directional?
- Is technical health investment visible alongside product work?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I build outcome-based roadmaps with clear horizons: near-term commitments, medium-term directional bets, and long-term strategic intent. I keep technical health investment visible and review roadmap assumptions quarterly."

## Common Mistakes
- Roadmap as complete feature list for the year.
- No buffer for discovery, incidents, or re-prioritization.
- Technical debt invisible to stakeholders.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Roadmap = strategic communication tool.
- Use outcomes not features.
- Leave space for learning and change.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you build a roadmap under uncertainty?
2. How do you say no to stakeholders while maintaining trust?
3. How do you handle roadmap reprioritization mid-quarter?
4. How much of a roadmap should be committed vs directional?
5. How do you make technical investment visible to business stakeholders?

## Key Takeaways
- Good roadmaps communicate intent, not certainty.
- Outcome framing improves stakeholder alignment and team autonomy.
- TPMs own roadmap communication as much as roadmap content.
