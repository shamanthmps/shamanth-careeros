# Product Discovery

## Executive Summary
Product discovery is the process of validating that you are building the right thing before investing full delivery resources. In TPM interviews, this topic evaluates whether you can design fast learning loops that reduce wasted engineering effort.

Strong answers show structured discovery — not just user interviews, but validated hypotheses tied to build/no-build decisions.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Discovery prevents costly delivery of wrong solutions.
- Architecture relevance: Discovery scope gates architecture investment size.
- Interview relevance: Common in product-focused TPM rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Problem hypothesis: Assumption about user pain to validate.
- Solution hypothesis: Assumption about what will solve it.
- Desirability: Do users want this?
- Feasibility: Can we build it sustainably?
- Viability: Does it create business value?

### Internal Working
Hypothesis -> smallest test to validate -> evidence collected -> build/pivot/stop decision.

### ASCII Diagram
Problem Hypothesis -> Experiment Design -> User Evidence -> Decision Gate

### Trade-offs
- More discovery reduces risk but delays delivery.
- Less discovery moves fast but risks building the wrong thing.

## Real-world Example
Before building a full ML-powered content recommendation engine, a team ran a simple manual curation experiment. Users did not engage differently. The team pivoted the hypothesis before significant engineering was committed.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What assumption are we testing?
- What evidence would make us stop or change direction?
- What is the minimum effort to learn what we need to know?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I structure discovery around explicit hypotheses and the cheapest test to validate them. I define decision criteria in advance so teams have confidence to pivot without politics blocking evidence-based choices."

## Common Mistakes
- Skipping discovery for "obvious" problems.
- Running discovery that never informs decisions.
- Treating user interviews as the only discovery method.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Discovery = validate before committing resources.
- Test cheapest hypothesis first.
- Pre-define decision criteria before seeing results.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you design a discovery experiment?
2. How do you prevent discovery from becoming a delay tactic?
3. What types of discovery evidence are most reliable?
4. How do you handle team pressure to skip discovery?
5. How do you know when discovery is complete?

## Key Takeaways
- Discovery is risk reduction, not bureaucracy.
- Hypothesis-driven discovery is faster and more decisive.
- TPMs should protect discovery time while keeping it focused.
