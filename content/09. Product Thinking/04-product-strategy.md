# Product Strategy

## Executive Summary
Product strategy defines where to play and how to win in a market over a multi-year horizon. For TPMs, strategic awareness means connecting program delivery to long-term product direction, not just executing quarterly roadmaps.

Senior interview answers demonstrate that you understand the "why behind the what" of your product area.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Strategic alignment prevents programs from optimizing locally but failing globally.
- Architecture relevance: Platform decisions should support strategic product directions.
- Interview relevance: Tested in Staff/Principal rounds and executive conversations.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Market positioning: How the product competes in its space.
- Differentiation: What makes the product uniquely valuable.
- Strategic bet: High-conviction investment in an uncertain outcome.
- Moat: Durable competitive advantage that is hard to replicate.

### Internal Working
Market context -> strategic bets -> product pillars -> quarterly roadmap -> delivery -> outcome feedback.

### ASCII Diagram
Vision -> Strategy -> Bets -> Roadmap -> Delivery -> Metrics

### Trade-offs
- Long-horizon strategy vs near-term pressure.
- Focus (few bets) vs optionality (many experiments).

## Real-world Example
A developer tools team aligned their infrastructure investment roadmap to a strategic bet on developer self-service, allowing product teams to launch faster without platform bottlenecks. This connected team-level delivery to company-level strategic intent.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- How does this program advance the product strategy?
- Which strategic bets are at risk if this program is delayed?
- Are we making tactical investments that conflict with long-term direction?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I ensure program delivery aligns to product strategy by understanding the company's strategic bets, then connecting my programs to those outcomes rather than managing feature lists."

## Common Mistakes
- Operating without knowing the product strategy.
- Treating all roadmap items as equally strategic.
- No feedback loop from delivery results to strategy assumptions.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Strategy = where to play + how to win.
- Programs should serve strategic bets.
- TPMs who understand strategy are trusted by executives.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you connect program planning to product strategy?
2. How do you challenge a roadmap item that conflicts with strategy?
3. How should TPMs engage in strategic planning?
4. What is a strategic bet and how do you evaluate it?
5. How do you communicate strategic context to your engineering team?

## Key Takeaways
- Strategic alignment is a senior TPM differentiator.
- Programs without strategic context risk high effort with low impact.
- TPMs should know the "why behind the what" in their product area.
