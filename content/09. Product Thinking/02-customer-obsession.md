# Customer Obsession

## Executive Summary
Customer obsession is Amazon's first leadership principle and a foundational evaluation lens at MAANG+ companies. In interviews, it means demonstrating that you start every decision with the customer and work backward.

TPMs who practice customer obsession drive programs with user trust and business resilience, not just shipping velocity.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Customer-obsessed TPMs challenge scope that does not serve users.
- Architecture relevance: System reliability and performance standards are customer commitments.
- Interview relevance: Explicitly evaluated at Amazon and implicitly at all MAANG+ companies.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Customer obsession: Prioritizing long-term customer trust over short-term internal convenience.
- Working backward: Start from press release and FAQ describing customer outcome, then plan backward to build.
- Customer proxy: Internal representation of user needs when real users are unavailable.

### Internal Working
Define customer outcome -> validate with data/research -> design delivery -> measure customer impact.

### ASCII Diagram
Customer Need -> Decision -> Delivery -> Customer Impact Measurement

### Trade-offs
- Deep customer focus vs internal team velocity pressures.
- Long-term trust vs short-term feature count.

## Real-world Example
A team was about to ship a performance improvement measured in milliseconds that would save infra cost. A customer-obsessed TPM pulled support data showing users were abandoning due to a confusing onboarding step — the team reprioritized.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What would the customer say if they saw this tradeoff?
- Are we measuring customer impact or just delivery completion?
- What does the support/feedback data say about real user pain?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I start every prioritization decision by asking what the customer impact is. I use support data, NPS, and session analytics to validate assumptions, and I challenge roadmap items that optimize for internal metrics at the expense of user trust."

## Common Mistakes
- Using customer obsession as a slogan rather than a decision practice.
- Ignoring customer data in technical trade-offs.
- Measuring delivery without measuring customer outcome.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Customer obsession = user impact before internal metrics.
- Working backward is a structured practice, not a mindset slogan.
- Every program decision should survive the "what would customers think?" test.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Describe a time you pushed back on a product decision to protect customer experience.
2. How do you represent customer needs in technical reviews?
3. How do you balance short-term customer asks with long-term trust?
4. What data do you use to measure customer impact?
5. How do you apply working backward in program planning?

## Key Takeaways
- Customer obsession is an operational behavior, not a value statement.
- TPMs demonstrate it through prioritization and challenge decisions.
- Interviewers look for concrete examples, not generic principles.
