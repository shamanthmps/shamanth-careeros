# Product Case Study

## Executive Summary
This case study integrates product thinking topics into an interview-ready scenario. Prompt: A B2C subscription fitness app has grown to 2M users but is seeing declining 30-day retention. How do you diagnose and address this?

Goal: demonstrate structured product thinking, analytics, prioritization, and execution clarity.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Mirrors real TPM ownership of product health and improvement programs.
- Interview relevance: End-to-end product thinking synthesis.

## Core Concepts
### Framework Applied
1. Define the problem precisely with data.
2. Form hypotheses about root causes.
3. Design discovery experiments.
4. Prioritize solutions.
5. Define metrics and launch plan.

### Diagnosis Steps
- Pull 30-day retention cohort curves by acquisition channel.
- Run funnel analysis through onboarding to first workout.
- Segment by user persona: beginner, intermediate, returning.
- Review NPS and support ticket themes.

### Root Cause Hypotheses
- Weak onboarding fails to establish workout habit in first week.
- Content library does not match beginner fitness goals.
- Notification strategy over-messages advanced users, under-supports beginners.

### Prioritized Solutions
1. Redesign day-1 to day-7 onboarding journey (highest reach, highest impact).
2. Introduce personalized beginner content tracks (medium effort, high retention signal).
3. Optimize notification timing and content by cohort (low effort, measurable lift).

### ASCII Roadmap
Diagnose -> Hypothesis -> Experiment -> Ship -> Measure -> Iterate

## Real-world Outcome Model
Expected outcomes:
- 30-day retention improves by 15-20% through onboarding redesign.
- Cohort analysis confirms improvement holds at 60-day mark.
- NPS improvement in beginner segment.

## TPM Perspective
Program governance:
- Weekly metric review for active experiments.
- Product, design, engineering, and data science aligned to shared OKR.
- Rollback plan for any A/B test with negative guardrail signal.

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I would start with cohort and funnel data to find where and when users drop off, form hypotheses about cause, run fast experiments on highest-impact areas, and measure outcome metrics — not just feature completion."

Why strong: structured, data-anchored, outcome-focused, and executable.

## Common Mistakes
- Jumping to solutions before diagnosing root cause.
- Treating retention as a marketing problem only.
- No experiment design before shipping changes.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Diagnose with cohorts and funnels before prescribing solutions.
- Form multiple hypotheses and test cheapest one first.
- Measure retention improvement, not feature delivery.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How would you diagnose declining retention in a consumer app?
2. How do you prioritize which retention lever to pull first?
3. How do you run experiments when sample sizes are small?
4. How do you communicate a product health program to leadership?
5. What does success look like 6 months into a retention improvement program?

## Key Takeaways
- Product case studies reward structured diagnosis over quick answers.
- Data, hypothesis, experiment, measure is the winning loop.
- TPMs who apply product thinking to health programs earn strategic credibility.
