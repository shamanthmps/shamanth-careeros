# Organizational Influence

## Executive Summary
Organizational influence is the ability to shape direction, culture, and decisions across an entire organization — not just within your immediate team. At Staff and Principal levels, you are expected to influence beyond your program.

Interviewers test whether you have operated at organizational scope or only at program scope.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Organizational influence is what distinguishes Principal-level TPMs from Senior-level ones.
- Interview relevance: Tested in scope and impact questions in senior rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Influence Mechanisms
- Scalable artifacts: Documents, frameworks, and tools others adopt.
- Communities of practice: Forums for cross-team learning and alignment.
- Coalition building: Aligning multiple stakeholders toward shared direction.
- Narrative shaping: Defining how the organization understands a problem or opportunity.

### Internal Working
Observe organizational pattern -> frame the problem -> propose scalable solution -> build coalition -> drive adoption.

### ASCII Diagram
Pattern Observed -> Framing -> Proposal -> Coalition -> Adoption -> Org Change

### Trade-offs
- Driving broad change takes time and attention away from programs.
- Narrow impact is safer but limits career trajectory.

## Real-world Example
A TPM noticed inconsistent release governance across 12 teams. They wrote a shared Release Readiness framework, socialized it through tech leads, got VP sponsorship, and drove adoption across the organization within two quarters. None of that was in their job description.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What organizational problems keep recurring that I could help address?
- How do I create influence at scale — through artifacts, forums, or coalitions?
- Who are the organizational nodes I need to win over?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I invest in organizational influence by identifying recurring problems, building scalable solutions, and earning adoption through relationships and demonstrated value. I think about my impact in terms of what changed organizationally, not just what I delivered on a specific program."

## Common Mistakes
- Limiting impact to current program scope.
- Building scalable frameworks nobody adopts.
- Organizational influence without organizational trust.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Organizational influence = scalable solutions + coalition + adoption.
- Principal-level TPMs shape org direction, not just programs.
- Artifacts that others adopt are leverage.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about your organizational influence beyond your program.
2. How do you build a community of practice?
3. How do you get a new standard adopted across multiple teams?
4. How do you influence organizational culture?
5. What distinguishes Staff-level impact from Senior-level impact?

## Key Takeaways
- Organizational influence is the Principal-level TPM bar.
- Scalable solutions and coalitions are the primary tools.
- Impact should be measurable at org scope, not just program scope.
