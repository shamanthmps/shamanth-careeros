# Leadership Fundamentals

## Executive Summary
Leadership for TPMs is about creating clarity, enabling others, and driving outcomes across organizational boundaries without relying on authority. In MAANG+ interviews, leadership is not evaluated through titles — it is evaluated through how you behaved and what you enabled others to accomplish.

Every senior TPM question is ultimately a leadership question: How did you influence? How did you decide? How did you build trust?

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs lead without formal reports, making influence-based leadership the primary execution model.
- Interview relevance: Leadership quality is evaluated explicitly in all behavioral rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Leadership: Creating conditions for teams to do their best work toward a shared goal.
- Servant leadership: Removing obstacles and amplifying team capability.
- Accountability: Owning outcomes, not just tasks.
- Psychological safety: Team members can speak up, challenge, and fail without fear.

### Internal Working
Leadership loop: Set direction -> Build trust -> Enable others -> Hold accountable -> Learn and improve.

### ASCII Diagram
Vision -> Trust -> Enablement -> Accountability -> Learning

### Trade-offs
- Empowering teams vs maintaining quality control.
- Moving fast vs building consensus.

## Real-world Example
A TPM leading a platform migration created a shared decision log, unblocked engineers proactively, and ran weekly alignment across 6 teams without being anyone's manager. The migration shipped on time with zero rollbacks.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Am I creating clarity or adding ambiguity?
- Which team members need unblocking right now?
- Who needs recognition to stay motivated?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"Leadership for me means creating conditions where teams can succeed. I focus on direction clarity, dependency removal, and building trust across functions so the work moves without needing me in every decision."

## Common Mistakes
- Confusing leadership with control.
- Leading only upward (for visibility) rather than across teams.
- No feedback loops for learning.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Leadership = clarity + trust + enablement + accountability.
- TPMs lead horizontally across functions, not vertically.
- Outcomes over activities define leadership quality.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you lead teams you don't manage?
2. What does servant leadership mean to you in practice?
3. How do you build psychological safety?
4. How do you hold others accountable without authority?
5. Tell me about your most challenging leadership experience.

## Key Takeaways
- Leadership is a behavior, not a title.
- Influence, trust, and accountability are the core tools.
- MAANG+ evaluates leadership through concrete behavioral examples.
