# Leadership Interview Guide

## Executive Summary
This guide consolidates the leadership topics most frequently tested in MAANG+ TPM behavioral interviews and provides structures, frameworks, and practice scaffolding to sharpen your answers.

The most important insight: leadership interviews evaluate judgment, not events. They want to understand how you think and behave, not just what happened.

## STAR Framework (Mandatory for Behavioral Questions)

Situation: Brief context — where, what, who.
Task: What was your specific responsibility?
Action: What did YOU do? (This is 70% of the answer.)
Result: What was the outcome and what did you learn?

### STAR Anti-patterns to Avoid
- Spending more time on Situation than Action.
- "We" instead of "I" — interviewers evaluate your judgment, not team credit.
- No Result or vague result ("it went well").

## Amazon Leadership Principles Most Frequently Tested for TPMs

| Principle | How It Shows Up |
|---|---|
| Customer Obsession | Starting decisions from user need |
| Ownership | Filling gaps, long-term thinking |
| Invent and Simplify | Removing process friction, novel approaches |
| Bias for Action | Moving fast with incomplete information |
| Earn Trust | Consistency, transparency, delivering on commitments |
| Deliver Results | Outcome focus, removing obstacles |

## Interview Archetypes to Prepare For

1. "Tell me about a time you influenced without authority."
2. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a decision and what you did."
3. "Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned."
4. "Tell me about your most complex cross-functional program."
5. "Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news."
6. "Tell me about a time you developed someone else."
7. "Tell me about the most ambiguous program you drove."
8. "Tell me about a time you drove organizational change."

## Story Bank Framework

For each archetype, prepare one strong story at three scales:
- Individual/team level.
- Program/org level.
- Company/strategic level.

This gives you flexibility to calibrate story scope to the interviewer's seniority.

## Interview Day Reminders
- Lead with conclusion, not with context.
- Name the specific leadership behavior you are demonstrating.
- Your values should be inferable from your actions, not stated directly.
- Pause before answering — interviewers respect thoughtfulness.
- Ask clarifying questions if the scenario is ambiguous — it shows judgment.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How would you describe your leadership style?
2. What is the most important leadership lesson you have learned?
3. How do you handle leading through uncertainty?
4. What is a leadership failure you are willing to discuss?
5. How do you prioritize when everything is urgent?
6. How do you keep teams aligned during organizational change?
7. What makes a TPM a strategic partner vs a task manager?
8. How do you earn trust in the first 90 days at a new company?

## Key Takeaways
- Leadership interviews are evaluated on judgment and behavior, not events.
- STAR structure with action emphasis is the baseline.
- Prepare a story bank across influence, ownership, conflict, communication, and change.
- The best candidates demonstrate multiple leadership principles in a single story.
