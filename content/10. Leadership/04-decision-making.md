# Decision Making

## Executive Summary
Decision making under ambiguity is one of the most evaluated leadership behaviors at Staff and Principal levels. Interviewers want to see structured reasoning, explicit trade-offs, and the willingness to commit with incomplete information.

Strong answers show a decision process, not just a decision outcome.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs must drive to decisions when teams are stuck and stakes are high.
- Interview relevance: Behavioral and case questions both test decision quality.

## Core Concepts
### Decision Types
- Reversible vs irreversible: Match decision process rigor to reversibility.
- Data-informed vs data-driven: Data informs but context and judgment decide.
- DACI: Driver, Approver, Consulted, Informed — a clean accountability model.

### Internal Working
Problem framed -> options identified -> criteria defined -> decision made -> communicated -> monitored.

### ASCII Diagram
Ambiguity -> Frame -> Options -> Criteria -> Decide -> Communicate -> Monitor

### Trade-offs
- Faster decisions risk errors; slower decisions risk delay costs.
- Consensus improves buy-in but can paralyze when stakes are low.

## Real-world Example
A team was debating between two architecture approaches for three weeks. The TPM called a decision meeting, assigned DACI roles, set 48-hour criteria review, and drove to a committed decision with a 2-week review checkpoint. The team moved immediately.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Is this decision reversible? If yes, decide fast.
- Who needs to be the Decider and are they empowered?
- What is the cost of not deciding?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I start by classifying the decision by reversibility and stakes, then frame it as a set of options with explicit trade-offs. I use DACI to clarify who decides. For reversible decisions, I bias for speed with monitoring. For irreversible ones, I invest in due diligence."

## Common Mistakes
- Treating all decisions as high-stakes.
- Decision by consensus when a single Decider is needed.
- No decision log or monitoring checkpoint.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Reversible decisions: fast + monitored.
- Irreversible decisions: diligent + explicit.
- DACI prevents decision paralysis and accountability gaps.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a time you made a hard decision with incomplete information.
2. How do you prevent decision paralysis in a cross-functional group?
3. What is your framework for reversible vs irreversible decisions?
4. How do you handle team disagreement after a decision is made?
5. Describe a decision you made that you later changed and why.

## Key Takeaways
- Decision quality is about process, not just outcome.
- Reversibility should determine urgency and rigor.
- TPMs drive to decisions; they do not wait for them.
