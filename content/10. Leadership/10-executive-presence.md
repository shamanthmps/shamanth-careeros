# Executive Presence

## Executive Summary
Executive presence is the ability to inspire confidence in senior leaders and across organizations through clarity, composure, and credibility. For TPMs, it is less about charisma and more about how you show up when stakes are high.

Interviewers evaluate executive presence through how you communicate in the interview itself, not just through story content.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Executive presence determines whether senior leaders trust you with high-stakes programs.
- Interview relevance: Directly evaluated by how you communicate in the interview room.

## Core Concepts
### Executive Presence Dimensions
- Communication clarity: Concise, structured, bottom-line-up-front.
- Composure: Steady under pressure, not defensive or reactive.
- Credibility: Depth of knowledge expressed with confidence.
- Gravitas: Seriousness of purpose and comfort with ambiguity.

### Internal Working
Prepare -> simplify message -> lead with conclusion -> support with evidence -> invite challenge calmly.

### ASCII Diagram
Complex Reality -> Simplified Message -> Executive Frame -> Confident Delivery

### Trade-offs
- Over-simplifying loses credibility with technical audiences.
- Over-detailing loses executives in the data.

## Real-world Example
A TPM presenting to a VP started with: "We are on track for the Q3 launch. The risk we need your input on is X." That 15-second opening gave the VP instant orientation, demonstrated situational confidence, and focused the conversation productively.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- What is the single most important thing this audience needs to leave with?
- Am I presenting this with appropriate confidence given my knowledge?
- Am I explaining too much or too little for this audience?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"Executive presence for me means showing up with clarity and composure, leading with conclusions, and maintaining confidence even when challenged. I prepare the 30-second version of everything before I walk into any executive conversation."

## Common Mistakes
- Over-qualifying statements due to uncertainty.
- Burying the point in background and context.
- Losing composure when challenged.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Lead with conclusion, then evidence.
- Composure under challenge is as important as preparation.
- Tailor message length and depth to audience level.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you prepare for executive-level communications?
2. Tell me about a time you had to present under pressure.
3. How do you handle being challenged on something you are not certain of?
4. How do you tailor your communication style by level?
5. What does executive presence mean to you?

## Key Takeaways
- Executive presence is developed, not innate.
- Clarity and composure are more powerful than polish.
- The interview IS the executive presence test.
