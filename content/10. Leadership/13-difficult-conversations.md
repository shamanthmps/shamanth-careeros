# Difficult Conversations

## Executive Summary
Difficult conversations — addressing performance issues, delivering bad news, challenging senior stakeholders, or surfacing uncomfortable truths — are a core leadership test. Senior TPMs who can have these conversations calmly and constructively create trust; those who avoid them lose it.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Avoiding difficult conversations lets program risks compound into crises.
- Interview relevance: Expected in behavioral rounds and evaluated in how you present trade-offs.

## Core Concepts
### Conversation Types
- Feedback: Addressing behavior impacting team or program.
- Bad news delivery: Communicating setbacks to stakeholders.
- Challenge up: Questioning a superior's decision.
- Accountability: Holding someone to a commitment they missed.

### Internal Working
Separate facts from story -> focus on impact -> choose right timing and setting -> deliver directly -> listen fully -> agree on next steps.

### ASCII Diagram
Facts + Impact -> Direct Delivery -> Listening -> Agreement -> Follow-up

### Trade-offs
- Directness improves clarity but can create defensiveness if not done carefully.
- Softening messages reduces defensiveness but obscures the real issue.

## Real-world Example
A critical vendor missed two milestones. Instead of softening the message, the TPM delivered clearly: "You have missed the agreed milestones twice. We need to understand the root cause and get a credible revised plan by Friday or we need to escalate to executive stakeholders on both sides." The vendor responded with seriousness.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Am I addressing the behavior or the person?
- What specific impact am I describing?
- What agreement am I trying to reach by the end of this conversation?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I approach difficult conversations by separating facts from interpretation, leading with the impact on the program or team, and seeking to understand before prescribing solutions. I choose directness over comfort, because avoiding these conversations costs more in the long run."

## Common Mistakes
- Burying the real message in politeness.
- Waiting so long the situation worsens.
- Making it personal rather than behavioral.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Separate fact, impact, and story.
- Direct + respectful is the goal, not direct OR respectful.
- Agree on next steps before ending the conversation.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a time you delivered very difficult feedback.
2. How do you challenge a VP decision you believe is wrong?
3. How do you have the conversation when someone is underperforming?
4. How do you deliver bad program news to executives?
5. What do you do when someone becomes defensive in a difficult conversation?

## Key Takeaways
- Difficult conversations are acts of respect, not acts of aggression.
- Avoidance compounds program risk.
- Directness, when done with care, builds trust rather than destroying it.
