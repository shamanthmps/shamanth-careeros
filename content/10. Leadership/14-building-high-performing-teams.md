# Building High-Performing Teams

## Executive Summary
High-performing teams do not happen by accident — they are built through deliberate attention to clarity, trust, healthy conflict, and shared accountability. For TPMs who lead without management authority, team health is created through culture, norms, and consistency rather than org chart leverage.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Program success scales with team capability, not just TPM effort.
- Interview relevance: Team-building questions appear in leadership and behavioral rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Team Health Model
- Psychological safety: Speaking up is safe.
- Shared goals: Everyone knows what winning looks like.
- Clear roles: No ambiguity about who decides what.
- Productive conflict: Disagreement is welcomed and resolved.
- Accountability culture: Commitments are tracked and honored.

### Internal Working
Set shared goals -> define norms -> build psychological safety -> enable productive conflict -> celebrate outcomes.

### ASCII Diagram
Goals + Safety + Norms + Conflict + Accountability -> High Performance

### Trade-offs
- Investing in team culture takes time early but multiplies output over time.
- Skipping culture investment is faster initially but creates friction later.

## Real-world Example
A new cross-functional program team struggled with mixed signals and low trust. The TPM ran a team kickoff to align on goals, created explicit norms around decision-making and feedback, and ran a retro after the first milestone. Within 6 weeks team velocity improved measurably.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Does everyone on this team know what success looks like?
- Is it safe to surface problems early in this team?
- Are commitments tracked and honored consistently?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I build high-performing teams by investing early in shared goals, clear norms, and psychological safety. I model the behaviors I expect — raising problems early, giving direct feedback, honoring commitments — and I run regular retros to keep the team improving."

## Common Mistakes
- Skipping team norms because the program is urgent.
- Psychological safety as a concept but not a practice.
- Ignoring team health signals until attrition or morale crisis.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Psychological safety + shared accountability = team performance.
- Norms must be modeled, not just stated.
- Retros keep the team self-improving.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you build a team culture when you are not the manager?
2. Tell me about a team you helped turn around.
3. How do you establish psychological safety quickly?
4. How do you handle chronic underperformance without management authority?
5. What are the early warning signs of a team in trouble?

## Key Takeaways
- High-performing teams are built, not inherited.
- Safety and accountability must coexist.
- TPMs build team culture through norms and consistent behavior modeling.
