# Change Leadership

## Executive Summary
Leading change — whether a technical migration, organizational redesign, or process transformation — requires more than project management. Change leadership is about understanding human resistance, building a compelling case, and sustaining commitment through uncertainty.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Most major programs involve significant change for affected teams.
- Interview relevance: Change management appears in transformation and scale questions.

## Core Concepts
### Change Dynamics
- Urgency: Why change now vs later.
- Coalition: Who is visibly supporting the change.
- Vision: What the future state looks like and why it is better.
- Early wins: Demonstrating momentum before full adoption.
- Resistance: Expected, addressable, not evidence of failure.

### Internal Working
Build urgency -> form coalition -> communicate vision -> remove blockers -> create early wins -> consolidate.

### ASCII Diagram
Urgency + Vision + Coalition -> Early Wins -> Adoption -> Sustained Change

### Trade-offs
- Moving fast on change risks losing key stakeholders.
- Moving slowly on change allows resistance to solidify.

## Real-world Example
A platform team migrated 30 services to a new deployment model. Initial resistance was high. The TPM secured 3 high-profile team leads as early adopters, documented the efficiency wins, and published them across engineering. Adoption accelerated dramatically in month 3.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Have I made the case for urgency compellingly and honestly?
- Who are my first three visible supporters?
- What wins can I demonstrate within the first 6 weeks?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I lead change by building urgency, assembling a visible coalition, and creating early wins that others can see and join. I treat resistance as information — it usually reveals an unmet concern I need to address, not opposition to defeat."

## Common Mistakes
- Announcing change without creating urgency.
- Treating resistance as a people problem rather than a communication gap.
- Declaring success before adoption is sustained.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Change = urgency + vision + coalition + early wins.
- Resistance is a signal, not a blocker.
- Sustain beyond first adoption to prevent backslide.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a major change program you led.
2. How do you manage resistance to change from senior engineers?
3. How do you build early momentum for an unpopular change?
4. How do you communicate a change that has no obvious upside for affected teams?
5. What causes change initiatives to fail?

## Key Takeaways
- Change leadership is human-centered, not just project management.
- Coalition and early wins are the highest-leverage tactics.
- Resistance addressed early is better than forced compliance.
