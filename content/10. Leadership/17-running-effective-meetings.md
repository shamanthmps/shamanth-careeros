# Running Effective Meetings

## Executive Summary
Effective meetings are a TPM leadership tool, not an overhead activity. Every meeting a TPM runs reflects their ability to create clarity, drive decisions, and use team time with respect. Running consistently excellent meetings builds a reputation that translates directly to stakeholder trust.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs run more cross-functional meetings than almost any role — quality matters.
- Interview relevance: Meeting facilitation examples signal leadership maturity.

## Core Concepts
### Meeting Types
- Alignment meeting: Build shared understanding.
- Decision meeting: Drive to a committed choice.
- Update/status meeting: Synchronize information.
- Retrospective: Learn and improve.

### Internal Working
Purpose defined -> right attendees -> agenda shared in advance -> facilitated to outcome -> decisions documented -> follow-up assigned.

### ASCII Diagram
Purpose -> Agenda -> Facilitation -> Decision/Action -> Documentation

### Trade-offs
- More structure in meetings improves outcomes but can feel rigid.
- Fewer meetings may feel efficient but misses critical coordination.

## Real-world Example
A TPM replaced a 90-minute weekly status meeting with a 20-minute decision meeting plus async written update. Attendance improved, decisions were faster, and team members reported higher meeting value.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Does this meeting need to exist or could it be async?
- Is the right decision-maker in the room?
- Will I close this meeting with a documented outcome?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I run every meeting with a purpose, an agenda, and a decision or action outcome. I distinguish meeting types so decision meetings never become update meetings. And I aggressively protect team time — if a meeting can be an email, I make it an email."

## Common Mistakes
- Meetings without a clear purpose or outcome.
- Missing the decision-maker while including too many informees.
- No follow-up on actions captured in the meeting.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Every meeting needs a purpose and outcome.
- Decision meetings and update meetings are different.
- Document decisions and assign actions immediately.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you run a meeting that is stuck and going in circles?
2. How do you handle a senior stakeholder who dominates meetings?
3. How do you decide if a meeting should exist at all?
4. What is in your standard meeting preparation?
5. How do you ensure meeting decisions get executed?

## Key Takeaways
- Meeting quality reflects leadership quality.
- Purpose + facilitation + documentation is the minimum bar.
- Protecting team time is a leadership act.
