# Coaching

## Executive Summary
Coaching is the practice of developing others by asking powerful questions, providing structured feedback, and creating space for them to find their own answers. Senior TPMs are expected to coach across functions and levels, not just direct reports.

Interviewers evaluate coaching through behavioral examples of how you developed someone else's capability.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs who coach build high-leverage relationships with engineers and PMs.
- Interview relevance: Expected in leadership maturity rounds and team development questions.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Coaching: Unlocking potential through guided reflection, not telling.
- Mentoring: Sharing experience and guidance directionally.
- Feedback: Specific, behavioral, timely observation linked to impact.
- Active listening: Fully attending to what is said and unsaid.

### Internal Working
Establish trust -> ask powerful questions -> reflect back -> identify insight -> support action -> follow up.

### ASCII Diagram
Trust -> Question -> Reflect -> Insight -> Action -> Follow-up

### Trade-offs
- Coaching takes more time upfront but builds durable capability.
- Telling is faster but creates dependency.

## Real-world Example
An engineer on the TPM's program struggled with stakeholder communication. Instead of telling them what to say, the TPM asked: "What outcome are you trying to create and what does your audience need to trust your judgment?" The engineer developed their own approach and used it successfully.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Is this person looking for coaching, advice, or validation?
- What question would help them discover the insight themselves?
- How do I give feedback that is specific enough to act on?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I coach by asking questions before giving answers. When someone brings me a problem, I first try to understand what they have already considered, then guide them to insights through questions. I reserve direct advice for when the stakes are too high for exploration."

## Common Mistakes
- Coaching as a disguised way of telling.
- Giving feedback that is too general to act on.
- Coaching only in formal settings.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Coaching = questions, not answers.
- SBI feedback: Situation, Behavior, Impact.
- Follow-up completes the coaching loop.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a time you coached someone to a breakthrough.
2. How do you coach someone who is resistant to feedback?
3. What is the difference between coaching and managing?
4. How do you give critical feedback without damaging trust?
5. How do you coach up — toward a more senior person?

## Key Takeaways
- Coaching builds team capability and earns deep trust.
- Questions outperform directives in long-term development.
- TPMs who coach create multiplied leverage.
