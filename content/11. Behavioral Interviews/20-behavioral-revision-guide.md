# Behavioral Revision Guide

## Executive Summary
This revision guide consolidates everything in the Behavioral Interviews module into a fast-review format for the 24-48 hours before your interview. It is not for learning — it is for activating what you already prepared.

## STAR Checklist (Review in 5 minutes)
- S: 2-3 sentences max. Context only.
- T: My specific accountability. Not the team's.
- A: What I specifically did. 60-70% of the answer. "I" not "we."
- R: What changed. Quantified where possible. What I learned.
- Target: 2-3 minutes per answer.

## Google Quick Reference
- GCA: Show your thinking process, not just the answer.
- Leadership: Influence without authority.
- Googleyness: Authentic, humble, collaborative, intellectually honest.
- RRKE: Show role-specific depth.

## Meta Quick Reference
- Quantify everything. Scale and impact are the currency.
- Bold decisions at speed.
- Large org, many teams, high business stakes.

## Anti-patterns to Eliminate Before Your Interview
- [ ] "We" when you mean "I."
- [ ] Starting with too much background.
- [ ] Result that is just "it went well."
- [ ] No learning in the Result.
- [ ] Answer over 3 minutes.
- [ ] Hypothetical ("I would...") instead of real example.
- [ ] Blaming circumstances or others in failure stories.

## Day-of Mental Checklist
- [ ] Story bank reviewed and titles activated.
- [ ] Three hardest questions practiced aloud this morning.
- [ ] STAR proportion reminder: Action is 70%.
- [ ] Leading with conclusion in communication.
- [ ] Composure prepared: pause before answering, think before speaking.

## Last 3 Questions to Practice Aloud
1. Tell me about a time you owned something well beyond your role.
2. Tell me about a time you had a major program failure.
3. Tell me about the most complex cross-functional program you managed.

## Key Takeaways
- Behavioral preparation is a muscle — practice aloud, not just mentally.
- Action proportion separates strong answers from average ones.
- Company-specific calibration (Amazon LP, Google attributes, Meta scale) is your last-mile prep.
- Walk in with your story bank activated, your anti-patterns eliminated, and your composure set.
