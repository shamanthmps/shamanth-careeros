# Tough Decisions

## Executive Summary
Tough decision stories demonstrate comfort with high-stakes choices under uncertainty, competing priorities, or significant consequences. At Staff and Principal levels, interviewers expect you to have made real decisions — not just recommendations.

The signal is not that you made the right call. It is that you had a principled process and you owned the outcome.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs must drive to decisions, not wait for them.
- Interview relevance: Every company tests judgment and decisiveness at senior levels.

## What Makes a Decision Story "Tough"
- Incomplete information with time pressure.
- Significant consequences if wrong.
- Stakeholder disagreement or competing values.
- No obviously correct answer.

## Story Framework
Situation: What was the decision context? What made it genuinely difficult?
Task: Who was the decision-maker? Were you the decider or the driver?
Action: What process did you use? What trade-offs did you evaluate? How did you decide?
Result: What happened? Were you right? If not, what did you own and change?

## Caution: What Not to Do
- Do not narrate a trivial decision dressed up as consequential.
- Do not show certainty that is not realistic — acknowledge the genuine uncertainty.
- Do not present a decision where you were clearly right with hindsight — show that you owned the risk.

## Example Story Frame
Six weeks before a major product launch, we discovered that a third-party compliance vendor would not meet the contractual readiness milestone. My options were: delay launch by 6 weeks, ship with reduced scope in a non-regulated segment first, or negotiate an exception from legal. The delay would cost $4M in contractual penalties. I facilitated a 3-hour working session with legal, product, and engineering, built a risk matrix for each option, and recommended the segmented launch with a 90-day roadmap to full compliance. I presented the option with full transparency about residual risk. Leadership approved. We launched on time in the unregulated segment. Full compliance was achieved in 74 days. The contractual penalties were avoided.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about the toughest decision you have had to make professionally.
2. Describe a time you had to make a decision with insufficient data.
3. Tell me about a time you had to choose between two priorities that both mattered.
4. How do you make a decision when your team disagrees with your direction?
5. Tell me about a decision you made that turned out to be wrong.

## Key Takeaways
- Principled process matters more than perfect outcome.
- Own the decision and its consequences fully.
- Reversibility and risk framing show senior judgment.
