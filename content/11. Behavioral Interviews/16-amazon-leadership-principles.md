# Amazon Leadership Principles

## Executive Summary
Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (LPs) are the explicit behavioral evaluation framework used in every Amazon interview round. Every behavioral question is designed to score one or more LPs. Knowing the principles is not optional — it is the game board.

Non-Amazon candidates should still study them: several MAANG+ companies use similar frameworks and Amazon's principles cover nearly all senior leadership behaviors.

## The 16 Leadership Principles

| LP | Core Meaning for TPM Interviews |
|---|---|
| Customer Obsession | Start from user need, work backward |
| Ownership | Own outcomes beyond your scope, think long-term |
| Invent and Simplify | Find creative, simpler paths to the same result |
| Are Right, A Lot | Good judgment, seek diverse input |
| Learn and Be Curious | Actively grow beyond current expertise |
| Hire and Develop the Best | Raise the bar; develop others |
| Insist on the Highest Standards | Do not accept "good enough" |
| Think Big | Bold vision beyond immediate scope |
| Bias for Action | Move fast with incomplete information |
| Frugality | Achieve more with less |
| Earn Trust | Be honest, transparent, and consistent |
| Dive Deep | Know the details; do not manage at altitude only |
| Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit | Challenge when you believe, then commit fully |
| Deliver Results | Outcomes matter, not just effort |
| Strive to Be Earth's Best Employer | Develop, include, safety |
| Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility | Think about societal impact |

## Most Tested for TPM Roles

| Priority | LP | Common Question Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Customer Obsession | Working backward, customer advocacy |
| Critical | Ownership | Gap-filling, long-term, accountability |
| Critical | Bias for Action | Deciding under ambiguity, moving fast |
| Critical | Earn Trust | Transparency, consistency, difficult conversations |
| Critical | Deliver Results | Outcomes, obstacle removal |
| High | Have Backbone | Disagreeing with a decision and committing |
| High | Invent and Simplify | Process improvement, creative solutions |
| High | Dive Deep | Knowing the numbers and details |
| High | Hire and Develop | People development, bar-raising |
| High | Think Big | Org-level impact, vision |

## Preparing LP-by-LP
For each of the 8 Critical and High priority LPs: prepare one strong STAR story and one backup story.

## LP Interview Prep Tips
- Amazon interviewers are assigned LPs to evaluate — your answer will be scored on that specific LP.
- Do not volunteer LP names in your answer. Just tell the story.
- Loop your answers to the LP theme even if the question wording is indirect.
- Prepare stories that demonstrate multiple LPs simultaneously — this is a senior signal.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a time you owned something that was not your responsibility (Ownership).
2. Tell me about a time you pushed back on a decision (Have Backbone).
3. Tell me about a time you had to move fast without full information (Bias for Action).
4. Tell me about the most complex program you delivered (Deliver Results).
5. Tell me about a time you were wrong and how you handled it (Are Right, A Lot + Earn Trust).

## Key Takeaways
- Amazon LPs are the explicit interview scoring rubric.
- Know all 16. Deeply prepare for the top 8.
- One story should serve multiple LPs — that is the most efficient prep strategy.
