# STAR Framework

## Executive Summary
STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) is the universal structure for behavioral interview answers. At MAANG+, every behavioral question expects a STAR-structured response. The framework is not optional — it is the interview protocol.

The most common failure is not knowing STAR. It is delivering it poorly: too much Situation, too little Action, and a vague or missing Result.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs answer behavioral questions in every round, not just HR screens.
- Interview relevance: STAR is the evaluation lens across all MAANG+ behavioral frameworks.

## Core Concepts
### STAR Components
- Situation: Set the scene briefly. 2-3 sentences maximum. Context only, no details.
- Task: Your specific role and responsibility. What were you personally accountable for?
- Action: What YOU specifically did. This should be 60-70% of your answer. Be specific.
- Result: What changed because of your actions. Quantify where possible. Include what you learned.

### Internal Working
Most candidates fail by inverting the proportion: they spend 60% on Situation and 20% on Action. Interviewers care about what you did — not what happened.

### ASCII Proportion
S (10%) + T (10%) + A (70%) + R (10%) = Strong STAR Answer

### Trade-offs
- Too short: interviewer does not have enough signal to evaluate you.
- Too long: interviewer loses the narrative and the key points.
- Target: 2 to 3 minutes per answer.

## Anti-patterns to Avoid
- Using "we" throughout. Say "I" — the interviewer is evaluating your behavior.
- Answering hypothetically ("I would...") instead of with a real example.
- Vague results ("it went well," "the team was happy").
- No lesson or reflection in the Result.

## Real-world Example
Weak: "We had a difficult project and I helped coordinate things and it launched on time."
Strong: "The Q3 platform migration was 3 weeks behind with 6 dependent teams blocked. My task was to own the coordination layer. I created a daily standby loop for the 3 bottleneck teams, personally unblocked the auth service dependency by escalating to the infra VP, and rebuilt the dependency map in 2 days. We launched 1 week late instead of 4. I learned that dependency maps need to be live artifacts, not documents created at kickoff."

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Walk me through your STAR framework structure right now.
2. Pick your strongest story and tell it in 2 minutes using STAR.
3. What is the difference between Task and Action in STAR?
4. How do you quantify a Result when you have no hard numbers?
5. How do you handle a behavioral question you have no direct example for?

## Key Takeaways
- Action is 70% of the answer — this is where interviewers evaluate you.
- Quantify results; if not quantifiable, describe the impact qualitatively.
- "I" not "we" — own your contribution explicitly.
- Every behavioral answer should include what you learned.
