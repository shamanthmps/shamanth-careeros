# Story Bank

## Executive Summary
A story bank is your curated inventory of career experiences mapped to behavioral competencies. The most common interview failure is not having the right stories ready — candidates with brilliant experience perform poorly because they are constructing stories in real time under pressure.

Build your story bank before interviews, not during them.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Senior TPMs need deep, multi-dimensional stories, not surface-level anecdotes.
- Interview relevance: One strong story should answer 3-5 different behavioral questions.

## Core Concepts
### Story Bank Architecture
- Depth: 6-10 strong stories that you know cold.
- Coverage: Map each story to multiple behavioral categories.
- Scale: Each major story should have a version at team, program, and org level.
- Recency: At least 2-3 stories from the last 3 years.

### Behavioral Categories to Cover
- Influence without authority
- Ownership and gap-filling
- Conflict and resolution
- Decision under ambiguity
- Failure and recovery
- Innovation and simplification
- Coaching and developing others
- Customer obsession
- High-stakes communication
- Program recovery

### Story Selection Criteria
- Real: Not hypothetical.
- Yours: You had primary agency.
- Specific: Concrete actions, not generic claims.
- Impactful: Meaningful outcome.
- Learnable: Contains a reflection.

### ASCII Mapping
Story A -> Influence, Conflict, Difficult Conversation
Story B -> Ownership, Failure, Recovery
Story C -> Innovation, Ambiguity, Decision

## Building Your Story Bank
Step 1: List your 15 most memorable career moments — good and bad.
Step 2: Tag each with behavioral categories.
Step 3: Pick the 8 with highest coverage and impact.
Step 4: Write full STAR structure for each.
Step 5: Practice telling each one in 2 minutes cold.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Walk me through your story bank — what categories do you have covered?
2. Which story demonstrates the most leadership behaviors simultaneously?
3. Which behavioral category do you have the weakest coverage for?
4. Tell me your strongest ownership story in exactly 2 minutes.
5. Tell me a story that demonstrates both failure and growth.

## Key Takeaways
- Six to ten deep stories beat twenty shallow ones.
- One story should answer multiple question types.
- Build, practice, and calibrate your bank before interviews begin.
- Recency and scale matter for senior-level roles.
