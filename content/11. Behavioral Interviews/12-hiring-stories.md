# Hiring Stories

## Executive Summary
Hiring stories demonstrate that you take the bar-raising function seriously — that you bring standards, rigor, and long-term thinking to evaluating talent. At MAANG+, the hiring bar is a cultural value, not just an HR process.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: TPMs often participate as interviewers, debrief leads, or bar raisers.
- Interview relevance: "Hire and develop the best" is an explicit Amazon LP. All MAANG+ companies evaluate judgment about team quality.

## What Strong Hiring Stories Show
- You maintained a high bar even when there was team or timeline pressure to lower it.
- You made a principled argument in a debrief that changed a hire/no-hire outcome.
- You improved the quality of an interview process you saw as insufficient.
- You recognized potential in candidates others were dismissing on surface signals.

## Hiring Story Framework
Situation: What was the hiring context and what was at risk?
Task: What was your role in the process?
Action: What specific judgment or intervention did you make?
Result: What was the outcome for the team, the hire, or the process?

## Hiring Anti-patterns Interviewers Probe For
- Hiring to fill headcount: "We were under pressure so we moved forward."
- No debrief influence: "I shared my feedback and the team decided."
- Recency and culture fit bias: Not catching pattern-matching on similarity.

## Example Story Frame
In a debrief for a Senior TPM hire, the team was leaning toward a hire but I had concerns about the candidate's ownership examples — they consistently used "we" and the situations were vague about their personal contribution. I raised this explicitly, walked through 3 data points, and proposed a second interview with a targeted ownership question. The second interview confirmed the concern. We passed on the candidate. Three months later we made a hire who outperformed expectations in her first quarter and is now being considered for Staff. I believe the bar-holding created space for the right person.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about a time you raised the hiring bar when others were ready to lower it.
2. How do you evaluate candidates for leadership potential versus current skills?
3. Tell me about a hiring decision you are proud of or regret.
4. How do you handle bias in hiring debriefs?
5. Tell me about a time you improved an interview process.

## Key Takeaways
- Hiring bar stories signal that you treat team quality as a long-term investment.
- Debrief influence, not just feedback submission, is what interviewers want.
- Passing on a candidate for the right reason is as strong a story as making a great hire.
