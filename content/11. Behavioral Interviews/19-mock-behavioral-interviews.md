# Mock Behavioral Interviews

## Executive Summary
Mock interviews are the highest-ROI preparation activity. Reading about STAR and behavioral frameworks produces awareness. Actually delivering an answer under simulated pressure reveals your real weaknesses: you run long, you use "we" instead of "I," your Result is vague, or your pacing is off.

This lesson gives you structured mock formats to use with a partner or alone.

## Mock Format 1: Solo Answer Drill
1. Pick a question from the list below.
2. Set a 3-minute timer.
3. Answer aloud — speak it, do not write it.
4. Record yourself if possible.
5. Replay and score: Was the Action specific? Did you use "I"? Was the Result quantified? Was it under 3 minutes?

## Mock Format 2: Partner Interview
Partner plays the interviewer. You answer. After each answer:
- Partner scores: Was the Action specific? (Yes/No)
- Partner probes: "Why did you do X specifically?" "What else did you consider?"
- Partner scores follow-up: Did you maintain specificity under probing?

## Mock Format 3: Rapid-fire Coverage Drill
Partner asks 10 questions in 20 minutes. You give 2-minute answers. Goal: ensure you can deliver across all behavioral categories without needing to construct stories in real time.

## Calibration Scoring Sheet (per answer)

| Criterion | Score 1-3 |
|---|---|
| Situation was brief (under 30 seconds) | |
| Task was clearly your personal accountability | |
| Action was specific, "I" not "we" | |
| Action was at least 60% of the answer | |
| Result was quantified or clearly impactful | |
| Result included learning | |
| Answer was 2-3 minutes | |
| Answer held up under one follow-up probe | |

Target score: 21-24. Scores below 18 indicate preparation gaps.

## Top 30 Mock Questions to Practice

### Ownership and Action
1. Tell me about a time you owned a gap that was not your responsibility.
2. Tell me about a time you had to move fast with insufficient information.

### Leadership and Influence
3. Tell me about a time you drove alignment across a highly skeptical group.
4. Tell me about the most complex cross-functional program you led.
5. Tell me about a time you changed an organization's direction.

### Failure and Learning
6. Tell me about your biggest professional failure.
7. Tell me about a time you made a decision you wish you could reverse.

### Conflict
8. Tell me about a serious conflict you resolved.
9. Tell me about a time you pushed back on a senior leader.

### Customer and Impact
10. Tell me about a time you advocated for the customer over internal interests.
11. Tell me about the highest-impact outcome you created.

### People and Teams
12. Tell me about someone you developed and what you did specifically.
13. Tell me about a time you built trust with a new team.
14. Tell me about a time you raised the hiring bar.

### Programs
15. Tell me about a program that was failing when you took it on.
16. Tell me about the most ambiguous program you have managed.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Conduct a full mock using Format 1 and score yourself.
2. Identify your 3 weakest criteria and target them in your next session.
3. Practice your 3 hardest story categories with a partner.
4. Time yourself on 5 different stories and check for pacing.
5. Practice handling the follow-up: "Tell me more about what YOU specifically did."

## Key Takeaways
- Speaking is different from writing. Practice aloud.
- Under-practiced candidates run over time or underdeliver on Action.
- Score yourself honestly — the rubric does not lie.
