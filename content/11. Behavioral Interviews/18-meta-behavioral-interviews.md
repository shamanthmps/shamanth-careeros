# Meta Behavioral Interviews

## Executive Summary
Meta evaluates candidates on four behavioral pillars: Initiative and Leadership, Impact, Communication, and Collaboration. Meta interviews also test for comfort with speed, large-scale ambiguity, and bold bets. The cultural signal they look for is "Move Fast and Build Impactful Things."

## Meta's Four Behavioral Pillars

| Pillar | Signal Sought |
|---|---|
| Initiative and Leadership | Did you drive it, or did you respond? |
| Impact | Was the outcome meaningful at scale? |
| Communication | Did you bring clarity to complex situations? |
| Collaboration | Did you work effectively across diverse stakeholders? |

## How Meta Interviews Differ
- Meta interviewers value velocity and scale. Small-scope stories do not score as well.
- Meta values bold action over careful consensus. Bias for action is highly rewarded.
- Stories should demonstrate large organizational scope where possible — multiple teams, millions of users, or significant business impact.
- Meta explicitly asks for impact numbers. If you do not quantify, you leave points on the table.

## Meta-Specific Behavioral Question Patterns
- "Tell me about a time you drove a large-scale program that required broad organizational alignment."
- "Describe a situation where you had to move very fast on an important decision."
- "Tell me about a time you created significant impact despite ambiguity."
- "How have you built consensus on a technically controversial decision?"
- "Tell me about a time you identified an opportunity others missed."

## What Meta Values vs What It Does Not Value

| Values | Avoids |
|---|---|
| Scale and impact quantified | Vague or unquantified outcomes |
| Bold decisions with accountability | Over-cautious, consensus-heavy process |
| Fast iteration and course correction | Long planning cycles without action |
| Direct communication | Political hedging |
| Cross-functional leadership | Silo-focused outcomes |

## Calibration Tips for Meta
- Quantify everything you can: users affected, revenue impact, time saved, engineers unblocked.
- Show organizational scope: reference the number of teams, the level of stakeholders, the scale of the problem.
- Demonstrate speed: show you moved fast and course-corrected effectively.
- Use direct language: Meta culture values directness. Hedged answers feel weak.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. Tell me about the highest-impact program you drove and how you measured success.
2. Describe a time you made a bold decision that required significant organizational buy-in.
3. How have you influenced a large engineering organization to adopt a new approach?
4. Tell me about a time you identified a major risk and moved quickly to address it.
5. How do you balance moving fast with maintaining quality on large-scale programs?

## Key Takeaways
- Meta rewards scale, speed, and bold action with measurable outcomes.
- Quantify impact explicitly — this is the highest-leverage prep adjustment for Meta.
- Cross-functional, large-scope stories score significantly better than team-level examples.
