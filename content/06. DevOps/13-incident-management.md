# Incident Management

## Executive Summary
Incident management is the coordinated process for detecting, mitigating, resolving, and learning from service disruptions. In interviews, senior TPM answers should show command structure, communication rigor, and learning loops.

Strong answers emphasize both response speed and prevention.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Incident execution quality directly affects customer trust.
- Architecture relevance: Runbooks and telemetry determine response effectiveness.
- Interview relevance: Common leadership and operations scenario.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Incident: Unplanned event degrading service.
- MTTR: Mean time to recovery.
- Sev level: Priority tier by impact.
- Postmortem: Blameless analysis and action plan.

### Internal Working
Detect -> Triage -> Mitigate -> Recover -> Communicate -> Learn

### ASCII Diagram
Alert -> Incident Commander -> Response Team -> Recovery -> Postmortem

### Trade-offs
- Fast mitigation vs deeper diagnosis during active incident.
- Frequent status updates vs responder cognitive load.

## Real-world Example
Payment outage:
- Established incident commander role and comms channel templates.
- Reduced confusion and accelerated coordinated mitigation.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Are roles clear (IC, comms lead, technical lead)?
- Are runbooks current and tested?
- Are postmortem actions tracked to completion?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I run incidents with clear command structure, impact-first mitigation, transparent stakeholder communication, and rigorous postmortem follow-through."

## Common Mistakes
- No incident role clarity.
- Delayed stakeholder communication.
- Postmortems without action ownership.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Incident response needs structure and speed.
- Communication is part of technical recovery.
- Learning loops prevent recurrence.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. How do you run a Sev-1 incident bridge?
2. What should incident updates include?
3. How do you prioritize mitigation vs root cause during outage?
4. What makes a postmortem effective?
5. How do you track incident program improvements?

## Key Takeaways
- Incident management is a core TPM leadership domain.
- Structure, communication, and follow-through matter most.
- Recovery and prevention must both be measured.
