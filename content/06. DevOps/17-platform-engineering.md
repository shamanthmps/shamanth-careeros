# Platform Engineering

## Executive Summary
Platform engineering builds internal developer platforms that provide standardized, self-service capabilities for deployment, observability, security, and runtime operations. Interviewers expect TPMs to distinguish platform engineering from ad-hoc DevOps support.

Strong answers show product mindset for internal developer experience.

## Why This Matters
- TPM relevance: Platform investment drives organizational delivery scalability.
- Architecture relevance: Standardized golden paths reduce operational variance.
- Interview relevance: High-value topic in Staff/Principal TPM rounds.

## Core Concepts
### Definitions
- Internal Developer Platform (IDP): Productized platform for engineering teams.
- Golden path: Recommended standardized workflow.
- Paved road: Secure, compliant default implementation path.

### Internal Working
Developer self-service portal -> templates/tooling -> automated policy checks -> deployment/ops integrations.

### ASCII Diagram
Teams -> IDP -> CI/CD + Runtime + Observability + Security Controls

### Trade-offs
- Standardization improves scale but may limit edge-case flexibility.
- Platform build cost vs long-term productivity gain.

## Real-world Example
A company created service templates with built-in telemetry and security defaults. New service onboarding time dropped from weeks to days.

## TPM Perspective
Ask:
- Which developer pain points are most costly?
- What platform capabilities should be self-service first?
- How is platform adoption and satisfaction measured?

## Interview Perspective
Strong answer:
"I treat platform engineering as an internal product with clear user personas, adoption metrics, and golden paths that improve developer flow while enforcing reliability and security standards."

## Common Mistakes
- Building platform features without user research.
- Over-standardizing and blocking innovation.
- Measuring platform output, not developer outcomes.

## 5-Minute Revision
- Platform engineering scales DevOps through productized self-service.
- Golden paths reduce cognitive load and risk.
- Success is measured by developer outcomes.

## Practice Interview Questions
1. DevOps vs platform engineering: key differences?

## Key Takeaways
- Platform engineering is a force multiplier for delivery orgs.
- Product mindset is essential for platform success.
- TPMs should align platform roadmap with business delivery constraints.
